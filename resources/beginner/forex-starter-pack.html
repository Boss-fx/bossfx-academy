01 What is Forex Trading?

Forex (Foreign Exchange) is the global marketplace for trading currencies. It's the largest financial market in the world, with over $7 trillion traded daily. Unlike the stock market, forex operates 24 hours a day, 5 days a week across global financial centers.

As a forex trader, you profit from changes in exchange rates between currency pairs. When you buy EUR/USD, you're buying euros while selling US dollars — betting the euro will strengthen.

02 Key Forex Terms

TermDefinition
PipThe smallest price movement in a currency pair (0.0001 for most pairs)
LotStandard unit of measurement: Standard (100,000), Mini (10,000), Micro (1,000)

LeverageBorrowed capital that lets you control larger positions (e.g., 1:100)
MarginThe deposit required to open and maintain a leveraged position
Stop LossAn order that automatically closes your trade at a specified loss level
Take ProfitAn order that automatically closes your trade at a specified profit level

04 Your First Strategy: SMA Crossover

The Simple Moving Average (SMA) crossover is one of the most reliable beginner strategies. It's the foundation of the BossFx SMA Pro Trend EA.

Setup

  • Apply a 50-period SMA and a 200-period SMA to your chart
  • Use the H1 (1-hour) or H4 (4-hour) timeframe
  • Focus on EUR/USD or XAU/USD

Entry Rules

  • Buy Signal: When the 50 SMA crosses ABOVE the 200 SMA (Golden Cross)
  • Sell Signal: When the 50 SMA crosses BELOW the 200 SMA (Death Cross)
  • Wait for the candle to CLOSE after the crossover before entering

Exit Rules

  • Stop Loss: Place below the most recent swing low (for buys) or above swing high (for sells)
  • Take Profit: Use a 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio minimum

05 The 5 Rules of Risk Management

  1. Never risk more than 1-2% per trade. If your account is $1,000, max risk = $10-$20 per trade.
  2. Always use a stop loss. No exceptions. Ever. A trade without a stop loss is gambling.
  3. Maintain a minimum 1:2 risk-to-reward ratio. Risk $10 to make $20. This means you profit even with a 40% win rate.
  4. Never move your stop loss further away. If price is approaching your SL, let it hit. Moving it is emotional trading.
  5. Step away after 2 consecutive losses. Review your journal, reset mentally, and come back the next session.
