01 Why Position Sizing Matters

Position sizing is the single most important skill in risk management. Two traders can use the same strategy — one blows their account, the other grows it consistently. The difference? Position sizing.

Getting the lot size right means you survive losing streaks and compound your winners. Getting it wrong means one bad trade can undo weeks of progress.

02 The Formula

Position Size Formula
Lot Size = (Account × Risk %) ÷ (SL pips × Pip Value)

Variable Breakdown

  • Account Balance — Your current total equity
  • Risk % — The percentage you're willing to lose (1-2% recommended)
  • SL in Pips — Distance from entry to stop loss in pips
  • Pip Value — Dollar value per pip per lot (varies by pair and lot type)

Common MistakeMany beginners set their lot size first, then adjust their stop loss to match. This is backwards. Always determine your stop loss based on the chart, then calculate your lot size from that. The chart dictates the stop — the formula dictates the size.
