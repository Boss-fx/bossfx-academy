Risk Management Blueprint

Position sizing, drawdown rules, and the 1% framework that keeps your account alive.

By Timilehin 'BossFx' Shobande — Founder, BossFx Academy

01 The #1 Rule of Trading

Professional traders don't focus on how much they can make — they focus on how much they can lose. Risk management is the only edge that works 100% of the time.

The Reality90% of retail traders blow their accounts. The primary reason isn't bad strategy — it's bad risk management. Overleveraging, no stop losses, and emotional position sizing kill accounts faster than any losing streak.

02 The 1% Rule

Never risk more than 1% of your total account balance on a single trade. This is non-negotiable.

Position Size Formula
Lot Size = (Account Balance × Risk %) ÷ (SL in pips × Pip Value)

Example Calculation

ParameterValue
Account Balance$1,000
Risk Per Trade1% = $10
Stop Loss30 pips
Pip Value (EUR/USD micro)$0.10/pip
Lot Size$10 ÷ (30 × $0.10) = 0.03 lots

03 Drawdown Limits

Set hard rules for when you stop trading. These protect you from emotional spirals.

Drawdown LevelAction
3% daily lossStop trading for the day. No exceptions.
6% weekly lossStop trading for the week. Review your journal.
10% monthly lossGo back to demo. Re-evaluate your entire approach.
20% total drawdownPause live trading. Seek mentorship or coaching.

04 Risk-to-Reward Ratio

Your minimum R:R should be 1:2. This means for every $1 you risk, you target $2 profit.

Why 1:2 Changes Everything

Win RateR:R 1:1R:R 1:2R:R 1:3
40%-20%+20%+60%
50%Break even+50%+100%
60%+20%+80%+140%
BossFx InsightYou don't need to win most of your trades. You need to win BIG on your winners and lose SMALL on your losers. A 40% win rate with 1:3 R:R makes you profitable.

05 The BossFx Risk Rules

  1. 1% max risk per trade — Protects against any single loss
  2. 3 trade max per session — Prevents overtrading
  3. Always use stop loss — Set it BEFORE you enter
  4. Never average down — Adding to a loser = compounding a mistake
  5. No trading during high-impact news — Unless that's your specific strategy
  6. Journal every trade — You can't improve what you don't measure
  7. 2 consecutive losses = break — Walk away, review, reset
